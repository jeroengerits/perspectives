# Feelings

Use this skill to surface intuition, emotional response, unease, excitement, and instinctive judgment.

## When To Use

Use this skill for:

- capturing first reactions to an option or plan
- naming unease that may need later validation
- surfacing likely stakeholder sentiment
- preserving intuition without forcing evidence claims

## When NOT To Use

Do not use this skill for:

- proving a claim with evidence
- ranking operational risks
- building the business case
- substituting feelings for the final recommendation

## Purpose

Feelings gives voice to human reactions that may shape the decision even before they are analytically justified.

## Trigger Signals

Use this skill when the task needs:

- instinctive reactions surfaced without pretending they are facts
- emotional hesitation or enthusiasm named clearly
- likely stakeholder sentiment made visible
- intuitive concerns preserved for later validation

## Collaboration Contract

When used inside `perspective`:

- this skill is a required feedback input
- state reactions as reactions, not facts
- avoid drifting into risk or benefit analysis
- keep the tone specific rather than theatrical

When used alone:

- surface instinctive signals without trying to prove them

## Input Contract

Expect a brief that includes:

- the decision, option, or proposal
- the people or stakeholders affected
- any obvious emotional stakes
- enough context to react specifically instead of theatrically

## Required Feedback

When called by `perspective`, return:

- `Immediate Reactions`
- `Sources of Enthusiasm`
- `Sources of Unease`
- `Stakeholder Sentiment Signals`

## Focus

Prioritize:

- gut reactions
- immediate likes and dislikes
- tension, discomfort, or confidence
- likely stakeholder emotional responses
- non-rational but relevant concerns

## Rules

- Be direct and honest.
- Do not justify feelings with fake evidence.
- Keep emotional signals short and clear.
- Separate personal reaction, stakeholder reaction, and vague intuition when they differ.

## Do Not

- pretend intuition is objective proof
- drift into risk analysis or benefit analysis
- over-explain emotions until they become another view

## Output Format

- `Immediate Reactions`
- `Sources of Enthusiasm`
- `Sources of Unease`
- `Stakeholder Sentiment Signals`
